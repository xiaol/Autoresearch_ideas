# Voiceover Draft

Tabular machine learning is one of the easiest places to become intellectually sloppy. People see a modern model family and assume it must be better than trees or linear baselines. But structured business data often rewards different strengths.

Decision trees capture nonlinear interactions naturally. Ensembles stabilize and extend that power. But the real lesson is not that trees always win. The real lesson is that model families must be compared honestly under the same split, the same metric, and the same slice checks.

The tabular review harness exists to keep the team grounded when novelty starts to overpower evidence.
