# Course Production Checklist

Use this checklist before recording, editing, and publishing each lesson.

## Lesson Design

- Confirm the chapter has one clear lesson goal.
- Confirm the lesson opens with one real case or problem.
- Confirm the lesson uses one main figure, not five competing visuals.
- Confirm the lesson has one reusable skill or harness to demonstrate.
- Confirm the lesson ends with one judgment takeaway and one exercise or reflection.

## Demo Readiness

- Re-run the planned command or artifact workflow before recording.
- Save the observed output in the chapter folder or example artifact folder.
- Verify every dataset path and script path still works from the repository root.
- Remove dead commands, speculative commands, or notebook-only assumptions.
- Keep the demo short enough that the viewer can understand the reason for each step.

## Visual Pack

- `assets/title-card.svg` exists for the chapter.
- A process figure exists and matches the narration.
- At least one screenshot or output capture exists.
- The visuals use the same chapter title as the book and course folder.
- The visuals show evidence, not only decoration.

## Voiceover

- The opening hook names the real problem in under 40 seconds.
- The explanation uses plain English before dense terminology.
- The demo segment explains why the command matters.
- The close names the engineering judgment, not only the tool.
- The voiceover sounds like teaching, not reading the chapter word for word.

## Editing

- Add chapter markers for the major teaching beats.
- Keep code and terminal text large enough to read on mobile.
- Trim pauses, repeated filler, and redundant restatements.
- Normalize audio level across chapters.
- Add captions or subtitles before publishing.

## Release QA

- Title, description, and playlist placement match the course plan.
- The description links to the book, examples, and skill references.
- The thumbnail or title card matches the chapter title exactly.
- The first pinned comment includes the main chapter resources.
- Watch the exported video once before upload to catch sync or crop problems.
