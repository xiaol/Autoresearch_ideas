# Voiceover Draft

Retrieval systems are especially dangerous when they look plausible. A few good examples can make us believe the system works long before we have inspected where it breaks.

That is why retrieval evaluation must be grounded in queries, relevance judgments, practical cutoffs, and representative failure patterns.

The retrieval evaluation harness helps us ask the right question: is the problem really the embedding, or is it the retrieval system around the embedding?
